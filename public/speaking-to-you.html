Speaking To You, aka My Phone, aka You

I used to be cynical about voice notes. There was something so awkward about them: slow to digest, hard to parse quickly, devoid of privacy when produced or consumed in public. But in the past year - a year spent a little too far away from some of my favourite people - I've come to appreciate the art of the voice note as a medium filled with intimacy, joy, playful accidents and sincerity.

I find myself playing and replaying these gentle, improvised performances from friends. Hearing their voice modulate through the whole range of their emotional moods. Voice notes present a conversation piece by piece. They lack the simultaneity of a phone call, replacing this instead with a series of back and forth artefacts, like notes passed in class. Tiny monologues about every day moments.

Speaking to you, aka my phone, aka you is a work about voice notes as a medium. The work consists of two parts. The first, presented above, is a single channel audio work. Collaging various voices notes in conversation with friends, the work covers topics ranging from migration & belonging, housing & making, to love & friendship. This is then accompanied by a written text included in issue 10 of Robida magazine, published in August 2024.

These voice notes are aural snippets of love: we hear love "in process", platonic love being reaffirmed. Conversations are a way in which we connect and reconnect with each other as social animals. This work is a testament to that process. To quote the endlessly quotable Ursula K Le Guin: "Love doesn't just sit there, like a stone, it has to be made, like bread; re-made all the time, made new."
